1. Preface

The Baron had fallen to the bottom of a deep lake. Just when it looked like all was lost, he thought to pick himself up by his own bootstraps.

—— Rudolph Erich Raspe

Just as the fun story above, bootstrap originally means that to get success without the help of others but just with their own efforts. In Statistics, the "bootstrap" method is to obtain new samples and statistics from the original samples. It should be said that bootstrap is a vary popular statistical method in modern statistics, and it works well in small samples. Many advanced algorithms have been derived from it.

2. A Well-Known Case

Let's start with this example of estimating the number of fish in a pond that everyone must learn in junior high school. Suppose there are totally 1000 fish in the fishpond, but now I don't know how many fish are in it. Then I want to count the number of fish in the pond. How can I count them? By catching all? Obviously it is not an appropriate idea. One method is sampling with replacement.

Steps:

  1. Block the fish pond off and do not let other fish get in (The overall distribution of regulations remains unchanged).
  2. Catch 100 fish and label them (Construction sample)
  3. Put the fish back in the pond and take a break for one night (Mix them into the whole population and make sure they are randomly sampled afterwards)
  4. Start fishing, 100 fish at a time, count how many fish are labelled, and what's the proportion (Resampling once for distribution).
  5. Repeat step 3 and step 4 for N times. (Establish distribution)

The principle of this method is the Central Limit Theorem(CLT). Let's assume that 100 fish are catched again for the first time, and 12 among are found with marks, with a probability of 12%. Put them back, and then catch 100 fish, and 9 fish are found with marks, with a probability of 9%. After repeating many times, you will find that there are about 10 fish with marks in each fishing. Therefore, we can roughly infer that there are about 1000 fish in the pond. Actually this is the idea of bootstrap. One more thing to consider: How about we use this method in an ocean with 1000000000000 fish rather than a 1000-fish-pond? Does this method still work?
